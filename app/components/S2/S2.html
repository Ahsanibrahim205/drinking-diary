How to keep a drinking diary

  • Print off a copy of the diary (on page 2) and fill it in for every day when you have a drink during the course of a week. (Alternatively, save the diary as a document and complete it on your computer). Most people find that it is best to fill in the diary each day at a set time, for example during a morning break after a day when they drank alcohol

How to convert your drinks into units of alcohol


One unit is equivalent to 8g or 10ml (1cl) of pure alcohol. The number of units in an alcoholic drink can be calculated by applying the formula Volume (ml) x ABV divided by 1000.
    As a rough guide:
  • One 175ml glass of 12% wine = 2 UNITS
  • One pint of normal strength lager (3 – 3.5%) = 2 UNITS
  • One 275ml bottle of alcopop (5.5%) = 1.5 UNITS
  • A single (25ml) measure of spirits (40%) = 1 UNIT

What To Do Next


Keep the diary for a week and then sit down and consider:
  • Are you happy with your alcohol use or is there something you’d like to change?

You may be unhappy with:


  • The amount you drink (either your weekly total or how much you consume on one occasion)
  • Particular occasions when you drink (e.g. when you have to work the next day, or are responsible for caring for a child)
  • The financial costs
  • Something else connected with your drinking
If you decide to make some changes to your pattern of drinking refer back to the Wellness Guide on Alcohol for guidance on managing consumption.
